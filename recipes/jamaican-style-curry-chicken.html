Jamaican-Style Curry Chicken

Description

Jamaican curry chicken is a popular dish that is enjoyed by many. It is a bold, flavorful dish that's cooked slowly over low heat, allowing time for the spicies to develop It contains pieces of juicy chicken, along with carrots and potatoes. The potatoes break down a little and help thicken the broth. Its bright yellow color comes from Jamaican curry powder. Scotch bonnet peppers give it a sweet, fruity heat. It's believed that the dish came to Jamaica in the 17th century when workers from East India were brought to the British colony

Ingrediets

  • Chicken
  • All Purpose Seasoning
  • Chicken Seasoning
  • Irish Potatoes
  • Carrots
  • Scotch Bonnet Pepper

Steps

  1. In a large bowl, combine the chicken with all the ingredients except the potato and carrot. Rubb the seasoning into the chicken to coat it well. Leave the chicken to marinate for at least an hour.
  2. In a large pot or Dutch oven, heat some oil over medium-high heat. Add the chicken and cook until browned on all sides.
  3. Add enough water to cover the chicken and bring to a boil. Reduce heat to low and let simmer for about 20 minutes.
  4. Add the potato and carrot to the pot and continue cooking until they are tender.
  5. Serve hot with rice or hard food
